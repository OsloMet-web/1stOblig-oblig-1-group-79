Smart cities

Smart city initiatives do not limit themselves to improving the efficiency of existing cities, but also aim to build completely new cities and neighborhoods. In 2016 Sidewalk Labs, a subsidiary of Google/Alphabet, proposed to build a completely new part of Toronto with smart city technology from the ground up. A steady stream of proposed smart city initiatives, such as Net City (China), Telosa (U.S), The Line (Saudi Arabia), arguably demonstrate the ambition to integrate digital technology into the city and bridge the gap between the virtual and the physical realms.
